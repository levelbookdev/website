Field records that hold up.

Tag the thing. Scan it with the phone in your pocket. Capture the work where it happens. What comes out is an append-only record you can hand over as an accepted deliverable.

Four tools, each covering one part of the job. Where a tool is done, we say so — a finished tool is not an abandoned one.

The tools

TrenchNote

Equipment and materials ledger. A QR code lives on the asset; scan it to see what it is, where it sits, and who moved it last.

Active · v1.0

trenchnote.com/Source

LoopCheck

Commissioning checkout, loop by loop. Every instrument already wears a P&ID tag; LoopCheck adds a QR for checkout status, punch items, and turnover packages.

Active · pre-release

Product page/Source

SlipSign

Same-day T&M tickets, signed on the tailgate before the crew rolls off site.

Stable · v1.0 · maintenance

Source

MainLine

Station-based records for linear work — depth, cover, offset, and a GPS fix at every station.

Dormant · feature-complete for shipped phases

Source

How they are built

  • One PocketBase binary running on your own hardware.
  • Static pages with no build step.
  • Offline-first, so the field never waits on a signal.
  • No accounts for field users.
  • Append-only records.
  • AGPLv3.
  • No trackers.

All four sources live at github.com/levelbookdev.
